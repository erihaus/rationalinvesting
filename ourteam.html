Manish Aurora

Managing Principal • Methodology and Product Architecture

Manish Aurora co-founded Rational Investing LLC and built its first valuations starting in 1998. The firm he established now consists of over 20 professionals who are responsible for modeling the G7 and MSCI World markets. Aurora also designed and developed the FX trading platform of FXCM (www.fxcm.com), which is now the world’s largest non-bank online FX dealer. Additionally, Mr. Aurora converted Merrill’s European FX derivatives exposure at the New York City, London, and Singapore offices to the Euro. At JP Morgan, Manish reprogrammed global swaps pricing and counterparty credit risk calculation using Massively Parallel Supercomputing technology. For the merger of Chase and Chemical, the biggest bank merger ever, Manish designed the Value at Risk calculator under a tight deadline from the Federal Reserve. At BlackRock, he designed and constructed its first CMBS and Corporate Bond credit risk models. Aurora worked as a sell-side analyst at Nomura Securities, where he covered real estate equity, debt, CMBS. Lastly, he built the first commercial paper direct issuance and investment management and reporting system for GE Capital, ITT, and Ford at Financial Sciences. Manish Aurora has an M.B.A. from the University of Chicago and a B.S. in computer science from the University of Scranton.

Harbhajan Aurora

Founding Principal • Technology and Research Management

At Rational Investing LLC, Harbhajan Aurora recruits and trains the analyst and software engineering teams in corporate finance in Bombay, India. With Manish Aurora, he co-founded Rational Investing and, since 1999, has overseen the firm's analyst team. He was instrumental in creating the valuation and data normalization processes of the company's equity valuation system. At FXCM, Aurora coordinated the development of the company's trading platform and oversaw its test team. He was also the principal of a textile manufacturing and trading business for 20 years in Bombay and Surat. While working at Chicago Pneumatic, he led the North Indian marketing and sales department, managing 30 professionals selling machinery for large-scale infrastructure projects of national importance. He received a B.S.E. from the University of Punjab, where he broke the state record for mathematics proficiency.

Pieter Hellquist

Principal • Fundamental and Valuation Research

Pieter oversees the review of 2,500 Standard DCF valuations produced quarterly at Rational Investing. He also oversees supporting fundamental research conducted by the offshore team. Previously in his career, he served as Head of International Operations for Velocity, a provider of electronic trading systems. Additionally, he was Global Product Manager of Citibank's cross border equities data and trading systems. At Citibank he also was Senior Manager for its e-commerce in Europe and FX trading systems out of Australia. In London, he was Marketing Manager of Information Products for the Northern Europe division of Reuters. During his time as Marketing Director in Japan, he built $100+ million (USD) practically from scratch. Hellquist received his M.B.A from INSEAD and his Masters in Engineering from Lund university in Sweden. He also studied for a year at the Ecole Centrale de Paris.

Daniel Savage

Systematic Trading and Fundamental Algorithms

Daniel Savage developed the trading platform for U.S. Interest Rate Options at a bulge bracket firm. He also advised the Prime Broker margin unit at Credit Suisse during the 2008 market event. At Prudential Insurance, he designed the credit risk management systems of the Global Derivatives unit. At Prudential Securities, he served as Senior Vice President of Corporate Risk. Savage also started as a runner at the AMEX for Timber Hill, the forerunner firm of Interactive Brokers. During the 1987 crash, Savage was trading the MMI futures in the CBOT pits. He later worked in the OEX pits at the CBOE and the FX Futures Pits at the CME. After earning his M.B.A., Daniel Savage joined First Boston in New York as a cash equities OTC trader. Savage received his M.B.A. from the University of Chicago and his B.S. from Northwestern University.

Iwona Bujak

Portfolio Systems and Trade Algo Testing

Iwona Bujak monitors U.S., European, and Japanese tracking portfolios of Rational Investing for corporate events and stops and coordinates all support for broker API's and risk reports. She is also responsible for testing all portfolio construction and execution systems, and performance measurements. Bujak started her career conducting market research on Enterprise Systems for D.H. Brown Associates. Iwona received her M.B.A. and B.S. in information systems from the City University of New York.
